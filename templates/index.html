📊 How Quality Score is Calculated

The quality score measures how well your ISP delivered service in that time window, compared to your best performance. It's a weighted combination of speed and responsiveness.

Component Formula Weight
Speed quality (median speed in window) / (90th percentile speed) 60%
Ping quality (best ping ever) / (median ping in window) 40%
Combined score (0.6 × speed) + (0.4 × ping) × 100% 100%
Example: If your 90th percentile speed is 400 Mbps, best ping is 2 ms, and a 3-hour window has median 380 Mbps and median ping 3 ms:
Score = ((380/400) × 0.6 + (2/3) × 0.4) × 100 = 83.7% (yellow: fair)

Color meanings:

Green (≥85%) — Excellent, you're getting 85%+ of peak speed with responsive ping
Yellow (70–84%) — Fair, some degradation but acceptable
Red (<70%) — Poor, significant quality loss; time to contact ISP

🔍 How to Read: "Where Time Goes Loading Each Site"

DNS (11–21ms) ✓ Our ISP — Looking up the website's address. Fast = ISP is routing well.
TCP/TLS (5–418ms) Connection + security handshake. ✓ If fast, ISP is good. ✗ If slow, website is far or overloaded.
TTFB (24–1188ms) ✗ Website's problem — How long the website takes to send back the first byte. Slow TTFB = website servers are struggling, not your ISP.
Bottom Line: If DNS & TCP/TLS are fast, your ISP is working great! Slow websites (Netflix, Stripe) are their infrastructure problem, not yours. 💪
